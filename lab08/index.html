Spaghetti and Meatballs: My Favorite Recipe

An original recipe handcrafted with love and care...

This recipe is an absolute classic. I love it because it is so simple and easy to make. The clean up is minimal and you can serve many people with it. It's my favorite because it is always a family favorite. Plus, you can always modify the ingredients to any degree that you like. If you need a delicious spaghetti and meatballs recipe that will have your family begging for more, look no further.

Here are the ingredients that you will need...

For the meatballs:

  • 1LB of Ground Beef (80/20)
  • 1LB of Italian sausage
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup of bread crumbs
  • 1/4 cup of grated cheese
  • Handful of Italian seasoning
  • A teaspoon of cayenne pepper

For the spaghetti:

  • 1LB of Spaghetti pasta
  • Salt for the water

For the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 3 cloves of chopped garlic
  • 1/2 onion diced
  • 1 28oz can of crushed tomatoes
  • 24oz jar of your choice of marinara sauce (Barilla - Garlic and Simmered Onion recommended)
  • About a handful of finely chopped parsley
  • Handful of Italian seasoning
  • Grated cheese (preferrably parmesan or mozzerella)

Instructions:

  1. We first begin by preparing the meatballs. Empty your ground beef and Italian sausage into a mixing bowl. Be sure to take the casing off the sausages. Crack in the egg. Put in a handful of breadcrumbs. Toss in about a tablespoon of pepper, salt, your Italian seasoning, and a dash of cayenne pepper for some extra flavor.
  2. Mix using your hands and combine all the ingredients together. Once complete, roll the meatballs into a ball and then coat all the meatballs in a thin and even layer of flour.
  3. Pour a tablespoon of high quality olive oil into a cast-iron skillet and heat over medium-high heat.
  4. Begin placing the meatballs into the pot and frying on each side for about 3-5 minutes. Once a brown crust begins to form on one side, flip all the meatballs and continue frying until all complete.
  5. Set aside the meatballs. Follow the instructions on the spaghetti package for boiling the spaghetti in a deep pot.
  6. Cook the spaghetti until al dente and then drain. Set aside to toss in the sauce later.
  7. In the same pot you fried the meatballs, put in the 1/2 diced onion and chopped garlic. Saute until aromatic. Then pour in your 28oz can of crushed tomatoes. Pour in your marinara sauce. Put in your Italian seasoning and grated cheese. Then mix together and stir.
  8. Allow it to come to a boil. Add your meatballs to the sauce mixture and stir thoroughly. After a few minutes, allow it to simmer on low, covered, for about 10 minutes.
  9. Finally add your spaghetti to the mixture and stir thoroughly. You can keep your meatballs and spaghetti separate if you like! Top your masterpiece off with some chopped parsely.
  10. Plate, and enjoy!
